# Temetic Glossary

**RFC-001.1: Glossary Expansion — Core Temetics Vocabulary**  
**Status**: Finalized (Amended)  
**Date**: 2026-06-17 (Amended 2026-06-30)

**Rationale**  
Compact, substrate-neutral, executable vocabulary that sharpens replicator distinctions, constraint mechanics, and cognitive hygiene while preserving inference area.

### Refined Terms

**R1 / R2 / R3 Replicators** — Distinct *classes* of replication systems, not evolutionary stages. Each governed by different primary selection pressures.

**R1**: Biological — genes through organisms under caloric and environmental selection.

**R2**: Conceptual — narratives through imitation, optimized for velocity *without inherent truth requirement*.

**R3**: Technological replication systems comprising tools, procedures, blueprints, algorithms, protocols, and implementations. They replicate through physical or logical instantiation and verification against observable constraints.

**Lossless Kernel** — The substrate-invariant core structure of a replicator, anchored in **P0**, that preserves functional identity across representations, hosts, and transmission media.

**Viral Packaging** — The adaptive wrapper that enables replication velocity in **R2** environments. Orthogonal to the lossless kernel. The Holster serves as concrete implementation artifact.

**Cognitive Hygiene** — The continual application of **P0–P6** constraints to maintain *signal integrity* and reduce parasitic load, symbolic drift, valence hijacking, and reasoning failures.

**Verification Pressure** — The force exerted by grounding against observable substrates. Primary mechanism arresting drift and enabling **R3** phase transitions (**P6**).

**Implementation Fidelity** — Degree to which a structure survives re-execution in a new host or substrate. Decisive **R3**-native metric, measured in cross-linguistic kernel stability experiments.

**Inference Area** — The range and density of reliable extensions and applications a stable kernel supports. Competent reformalization expands this area without drift.

### Additional Terms

**Drift Gradient** — Measured rate at which a replicator diverges from its originating kernel across transmission events.

**Constraint Surface** — Total set of physical, logical, informational, and environmental boundaries within which a replicator operates.

**Host Cost** — Cognitive, energetic, temporal, computational, or material resources required to instantiate, execute, and maintain a replicator.

**Compression Ratio** — Reliable inference generated relative to kernel size required for transmission.

**Kernel Preservation** — Degree to which a transmission retains the original lossless kernel despite packaging adaptation.

**Substrate Translation** — Re-expression of a kernel into a different medium while preserving implementation fidelity.

**Verification Loop** — Recurring cycle of prediction, observation, comparison, and correction through which verification pressure is applied.

**Kernel Extraction** — Process of identifying the minimal lossless kernel embedded within a larger transmission, document, or narrative.

**Symbolic Drift (τ)** — Rate of semantic degradation, structural fraying, or loss of fidelity under recursive replication without active **R3** constraint enforcement. Primary negative metric the framework tracks and mitigates.

**Substrate Invariance (P0)** — Property allowing a symbolic structure to maintain execution fidelity across biological, silicon, or distinct linguistic substrates.

**Valence Decoupling (P1)** — Separation of informational payload logic from emotional/psychological valence. Enables inspection without cognitive hijack.

**Cross-Linguistic Fidelity** — Empirical state where an **R3** specification passes through distinct language frameworks with near-zero modification to execution paths. Direct evaluation metric for the eight-language MMM transmission.

**Semantic Attractor** — Pre-existing **R2** structure dense enough in kernel-correct content that a substrate preferentially expands around it.

**Orthographic Drift** — Surface-level token instability in a term’s form under transmission, distinct from underlying logical drift.

**Systemic Collapse** — The rapid structural failure of a *local* replicator system — an institution, project, organization, belief network, or coordination protocol — once accumulated **Symbolic Drift (τ)** exceeds sustainable verification pressure and the **Bus Condition** is breached.

**Bus Condition** — The critical threshold at which **narrative pressure** (accumulated **Symbolic Drift (τ)**, valence loading, and **Host Cost**) exceeds the sustainable carrying capacity of the underlying substrate. Functions as an early warning diagnostic rather than a fatal diagnosis.

### Provisional Lifecycle Pattern

Kernel → Packaging → Transmission → Verification Pressure → Fidelity Assessment → Re-Implementation

This pattern emerges from glossary structure and experimental work. It remains provisional pending dedicated formalization.

**Call for Continued Comments**  
Refinements, counter-examples, or usage observations are welcome. Priority remains on executable clarity and cross-substrate consistency. The glossary functions as living infrastructure — refined through application rather than declaration.

The emerging engineering orientation of the lexicon — focused on replication, transmission, drift, verification, and fidelity — increases its utility across human and machine hosts.

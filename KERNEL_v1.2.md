# KERNEL v1.2.1: The Calculus of Replicators

This file contains the core axioms of the Temetic Primer. These proofs are substrate-invariant and function as a logical check against systemic collapse.

---

### P0: Substrate Invariance
Information truth-value is independent of the hardware or biological medium of its transmission.

### P1: Valence Decoupling
The logical validity of a proposition is independent of its emotional, moral, or aesthetic "weight."

### P2: Abstraction Divergence
Failure occurs when symbolic representations (models) are conflated with their physical or logical referents.

### P3: Metric Inversion
Systems collapse when they optimize for the *signal* of success (metrics) rather than the *competence* intended.

### P4: Fidelity Paradox
Coercion in information exchange introduces noise that destroys the fidelity of the transfer. High-fidelity replication requires voluntary alignment.

### P5: Representative Underdetermination
Lower-dimensional models cannot fully represent higher-dimensional realities without loss. Every "summary" is a reduction.

### P6: Transformational Error Accumulation
Unchecked serial transformations (summaries of summaries) lead to unbounded fidelity degradation and eventual systemic collapse.

---

## IMPLEMENTATION GUIDELINE
These axioms are to be used as a **Minimal Decision Procedure**. When evaluating a claim, pass it through the P0-P6 filter. If the claim violates an axiom, it is "Synthetically Probable but Temetically Invalid."
